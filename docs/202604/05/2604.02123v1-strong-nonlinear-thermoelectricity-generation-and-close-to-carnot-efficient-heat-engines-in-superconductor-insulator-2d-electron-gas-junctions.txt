## Strong nonlinear thermoelectricity generation and close-to-Carnot efficient heat engines in Superconductor-Insulator-2D electron gas junctions 

Leonardo Lucchesi ∗ and Federico Paolucci 

Dipartimento di Fisica, Universit` a di Pisa, Largo Bruno Pontecorvo 3, 56127 Pisa, PI, Italy and INFN Sezione di Pisa, Largo Bruno Pontecorvo 3, 56127 Pisa, PI, Italy 

We find that a novel Superconductor-Insulator-2D electron gas tunnel junction (SISm) strongly and efficiently generates thermoelectricity via a nonlinear mechanism. We simulate across the pa-rameter space of the junction, finding and discussing different regimes with features useful for thermoelectricity generation or for specific applications. The generated Seebeck potential can go up to 6 .75∆ 0 with a huge nonlinear Seebeck coefficient, and efficiency can get very close to Carnot efficiency η = 0 .96 ηC , a record for a solid-state device model. Thermoelectric performance is far better than analogous junctions, with fewer fabrication challenges, as the device can be fabricated via standard methods. 

In the increasingly important context of quantum tech-nologies [1], thermoelectricity in superconducting struc-tures turned from a curiosity into a promising framework for quantum devices [2–4]. The recent quantum revolu-tion is driven by development of quantum computation [1, 5] and quantum detection technologies [6, 7]. One of the main current limits for scaling solid-state imple-mentations for both applications is heat management in the cryogenic setup. The design of a scalable quantum computer must move from needing a coaxial cable per qubit towards the use of electronics working at the cryo-genic stage to reduce complexity and heat influx [8–13]. Scaling an array of superconducting detectors brings an analogous problem, as they usually require separate wires for biasing and readout [2, 14, 15], where only the read-out wiring can be reduced via multiplexing [15, 16]. Generating thermoelectricity in a cryogenic environment offers an unexpected path for solving these problems, as it can passively generate a detection or control sig-nal with no need for biasing, only by using a byprod-uct of quantum devices: heat. With semiconductors becoming inefficient at cryogenic temperatures [17, 18] and superconductors notoriously showing negligible ther-moelectric behaviour [19], superconducting hybrid struc-tures appear as the most promising framework for cryo-genic thermoelectricity generation [3, 20, 21]. The break-ing of e-h symmetry required for thermoelectricity can happen spontaneously, as in superconductor-insulator-superconductor (SIS) junctions [3], or explicitly, as in ferromagnetic-insulator-superconductor (FIS) junctions [20], fluxon-based [22], and GIS [21] junctions. The gen-erated thermoelectricity can be linear [20, 21], i.e., pro-portional to the temperature gradient across the struc-ture, or nonlinear [3, 21, 23]. Current junction designs, while promising, also imply important technical chal-lenges, as a SIS junction needs supercurrent suppression to keep a voltage gradient across it, and a FIS junction requires the complex fabrication of a high-quality ferro-magnetic insulator.

In this work, we introduce the Superconductor-Insulator-2D electron gas junction (SISm, where Sm stands for semiconductor) as a new system for cryogenic thermoelectricity generation. The band structure of an SISm junction explicitly breaks e-h symmetry, generat-ing a quasiparticle current from a temperature gradient. A SISm junction addresses the main problems of current designs, as it does not need supercurrent suppression to generate a voltage gradient as a SIS junction [3] and, in principle, it can be fabricated by using standard su-perconductor and 2DEG fabrication techniques, for ex-ample those used for high electron mobility transistors (HEMT) [24, 25]. As proven in Mahan and Sofo [29], generating thermo-electricity in narrow energy intervals maximizes the fig-ures of merit, and the work of Jordan et al. [30] proves that the efficiency of a heat engine made by a tunnel junction with a δ-shaped transmission function can tend to the Carnot efficiency (see the discussion on stopping voltage). We believe our system to be a close, solid-state realization of the concept described in [30], which justifies the simulated high efficiency. Increasing the temperature to TS = 0 .9Tc with Ec = 0 .03∆ 0 allows a compromise, with W ∼ 0.02 GT (∆ 0/e )2 and η = 0 .8ηC for V = 2∆ 0/e ,as shown in the SM [28]. 

Conclusions In this work, we showed the physics of the strong thermoelectricity generation in a superconductor-insulator-2D electron gas (SISm) junction and its po-tential for cryogenic applications. In separate cases, the junction generates an open-circuit potential up to 

VS ∼ 6.75∆ 0/e (1 .35 mV for Al) with maximum nonlin-ear Seebeck coefficient S ∼ 1 mV /K for Al, and a closed-circuit current up to I0 ∼ 0.12 GT ∆0/e . As a heat en-gine, the junction is able to generate a power of W ∼

0.1GT (∆ 0/e )2 (∼ 4 pW for Al with tunnel resistance 

RT = 1 kΩ). The maximum efficiency is close to the Carnot efficiency η = 0 .96 ηC . To the best of our knowl-edge, this junction is the electronic system closest to a re-versible heat engine. Thermoelectric figures of merit are similar or much better than analogous junctions [3, 20– 522], with fewer fabrication challenges due to the possibil-ity of using standard 2DEG fabrication techniques. Dif-ferent regimes of the junction bring several features with potential for specific applications. A bistable state in an interval of TS for Ec < −∆0 could be used as a ther-mal memory or a thermal switch (see conclusions of [3]). The system also presents two regimes where VS depends strongly on TS, the basis of a responsive thermometer [6] or a sensitive radiation detector such as a bolometer, as for an open-circuit bolometer the ideal responsivity can be estimated as δV S /δP in = ( δV S /δT S) × (δT S/δP in ), where Pin is the detected radiation power [31].
