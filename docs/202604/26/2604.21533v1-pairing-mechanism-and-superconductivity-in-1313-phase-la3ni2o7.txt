Recently, the observation of superconductivity (SC) with Tc ≈ 3.6 K in the pressurized 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 has attracted considerable interest. Here, we systematically investigate the electronic properties and superconducting mechanism of 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 using density functional theory plus dynamical mean-field theory (DFT+DMFT) and random phase approximation (RPA). Our DFT+DMFT calculations reveal that the single-layer (SL) subsystem exhibits nearly insulating behavior, with the dz2 orbital showing Mott physics, while the trilayer (TL) subsystem remains metallic. This indicates that SC primarily resides in the TL subsystem, whose Ni-eg orbitals are found to be hole-doped relative to bulk La 4Ni 3O10 . Based on DFT+DMFT-derived low-energy Hamiltonian, RPA-based analysis yields an s±-wave pairing symmetry within the TL subsystem. Importantly, we identify two key factors that contribute to the significant suppression of Tc in 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 compared to bulk La 4Ni 3O10 . First, the hole doping in the TL subsystem, as estab-lished by DMFT, leads to a decreased pairing strength, as confirmed by RPA calculations—a trend resembling that in bulk La 4Ni 3O10 . Second, the SL subsystem acts as a bridge connecting adja-cent superconducting TL subsystems, thereby forming an S-N-S Josephson junction. The resulting interlayer Josephson coupling governs the phase coherence between TL subsystems and further sup-presses the global Tc. Combinedly, our findings suggest that the high-Tc phase in the RP La 3Ni 2O7

family should be attributed to the 2222 La 3Ni 2O7 rather than the 1313 La 3Ni 2O7.

Introduction. Since the discovery of superconductiv-ity (SC) above the liquid-nitrogen temperature in pres-surized La 3Ni 2O7 [1], nickelates have emerged as a hot research focus in condensed matter physics, triggering an intense surge of both theoretical and experimental investigations [2–23]. Subsequently, the observation of SC in pressurized La 4Ni 3O10 , ambient-pressure thin-film La 3Ni 2O7, and doped La 3Ni 2O7 has further cat-alyzed research in this field, establishing nickelates as an important platform for realizing high-Tc SC [24–29]. In the Ruddlesden-Popper (RP) phases La 3Ni 2O7 and La 4Ni 3O10 , each Ni atom is octahedrally coordinated by O atoms. Under ambient pressure, these octahedra ex-hibit tilting or rotation. Upon increasing external pres-sure, the system undergoes a structural phase transition that straightens the interlayer Ni–O–Ni bond angles to 180 ◦, accompanied by the metallization of the σ-bonding states. Furthermore, whether SC can also be realized in hy-brid RP nickelates such as the 1212 La 5Ni 3O11 , 1313 La 3Ni 2O7, and 2323 La 7Ni 5O17 phases has become an important question of interest [30–34]. To date, both 1212 La 5Ni 3O11 , 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 and 2323 La 7Ni 5O17 

have been successfully synthesized experimentally [31– 33, 35]. In particular, the 1212 La 5Ni 3O11 exhibits SC

Moreover, angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (ARPES) experiments re-veal that, near the Fermi level, only bands originat-ing from the bilayer subsystem are observed in thin-film 1212 La 5Ni 3O11 [35]. However, there is ongoing theoretical debate regarding the pairing mechanism in 1212 La 5Ni 3O11 . A random phase approximation (RPA)-based study attributed the SC to the single-layer (SL) subsystem, but the resulting pairing strength is too weak to account for the experimentally observed high Tc [37]. Other works suggest that SC in 1212 La 5Ni 3O11 arises primarily from the bilayer subsystem [34, 38, 39], with the SL subsystem forming a Josephson coupling structure with the bilayer subsystem, which explains the dome-shaped dependence of Tc observed in experiments [38]. Recently, the SC of 1313 phase of La 3Ni 2O7 has at-tracted significant attention. A previous experimental study has reported the observation of filamentary SC up to 80 K in 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 via high-pressure transport measurements [32]. However, it was noted that the pos-sibility of high-Tc arising from other phases, such as 2222 La 3Ni 2O7, could not be ruled out. More recently, new ex-perimental work has demonstrated that 1313 La 3Ni 2O7

exhibits SC with a maximum Tc of 3.6 K above 19 GPa, accompanied by a pressure induced structural phase tran-sition at 13 GPa [40]. Recently, thin-film experiments have revealed that while the 2222 phase of La 3Ni 2O7 can manifest SC, the 1313 phase does not [35, 41], casting further doubt on the intrinsic SC of 1313 La 3Ni 2O7. An earlier RPA-based study proposed that the SL subsystem hosts a leading dx2−y2 pairing state, with the nonsuperconduct-ing trilayer (TL) subsystem in-between [42]. In contrast, density functional theory plus dynamical mean-field the-ory (DFT+DMFT) investigations have revealed that the SL subsystem exhibits Mott insulating behavior, simi-lar to that of the 1212 La 5Ni 3O11 , rendering it an un-likely host for superconductivity [43, 44]. Therefore, a comprehensive understanding of the electronic and su-perconducting properties of 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 becomes an urgent and crucial issue in the field. In this work, we systematically study the electronic properties using DFT+DMFT calculations, followed by an RPA based analysis to investigate the supercon-ducting mechanism in hybrid 1313 La 3Ni 2O7. Our DMFT results reveal a layer-dependent correlation effect, where the SL subsystem manifests as a nearly insulat-ing bad metal, while the TL subsystem remains metallic and features hole-doped Ni-eg orbitals relative to bulk La 4Ni 3O10 . Using DFT+DMFT derived effective model, our RPA analysis identifies an s±-wave pairing symme-try in the TL subsystem of 1313 La 3Ni 2O7. The pairing strength within the TL subsystem shows a similar dop-ing trend to bulk La 4Ni 3O10 , which decreases upon hole doping, implying a lower Tc compared to bulk La 4Ni 3O10 .Additionally, the SL and TL subsystems form an S-N-S Josephson junction, where the SL subsystem acts as a weak link, suppressing phase coherence and leading to a significantly reduced global Tc. Our findings clarify the genuine high-Tc phase in RP La 3Ni 2O7 series and high-light the important difference between hybrid-phase and pure-phase nickelate superconductors. 

Electronic correlation. Hybrid 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 fea-tures an alternating stacking structure composed of SL La 2NiO 4 and TL La 4Ni 3O10 subsystems, with a transla-tion of ( 12 , 12 ) between SL and TL subsystems, as shown in Fig.1. Each Ni is surrounded by six oxygen atoms, form-ing an octahedron. At ambient pressure, 1313 La 3Ni 2O7

crystallizes in the orthorhombic Cmmm space group [40]. By applying external pressure, a structural phase tran-sition occurs at approximately 13 GPa, leading to a tetragonal phase with P 4/mmm space group. Distinct from the 2222 La 3Ni 2O7 and TL La 4Ni 3O10 , hybrid 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 exhibits no octahedral tilt or rotation of the NiO 6 octahedron at ambient pressure. Throughout the pressure-induced structural phase transition, the out-of-plane Ni-O-Ni bond angle within the TL subsystem re-mains 180 ◦, analogous to the hybrid 1212 La 5Ni 3O11 [36]. Figure 2(a) presents the fully self-consistent DFT+DMFT calculated correlated electronic structure of the 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 at 20 GPa. The momentum-resolved spectral function A(k, ω ) reveals pronounced band renormalization effects, with the Ni-eg bandwidth reduced from 4 eV in DFT calculation to approximately 2.5 eV in DMFT calculation, indicating strong electronic correlations in the 1313 La 3Ni 2O7. Around the M point, the bands originating from the TL subsystem become flattened due to renormalization effects, and the bonding band slightly crosses the Fermi level, leading to the γ

pocket on the FS. To further elucidate the characteristics of the bands near the FS, Figure 2(b) and (c) show the orbital-resolved imaginary part of Matsubara self-energy func-tion Im P(iω n) and orbital-resolved spectral functions

Specifically, the SL-dz2 orbital manifests Mott physics, whereas the SL-dx2−y2 orbital displays a large intercept at zero-frequency, indicating the intrinsic strong correlations of the SL subsystem. This is consistent with the well-known Mott-insulating nature of La 2NiO 4 [45–47]. The local spectral functions A(k, ω ) of the SL subsystem ex-hibit a pronounced difference from those of TL subsys-tem near the Fermi level. Note that the dx2−y2 orbital exhibits a tiny spectral function intensity in the vicin-ity of the Fermi energy. However, the spectrum is dis-persed throughout the entire momentum space, without manifesting well-defined quasiparticle excitations, as il-lustrated in Fig.2(a). Therefore, the SL subsystem be-haves as a nearly insulating bad metal, characterized by a gap near the Fermi level and the large imaginary part of self-energy, consistent with previous studies [43, 44]. To understand the underlying ori-gin of this behavior, let us return to the FS nesting in Fig. 4(b). Around the Γ point, the electron-like ε pocket is smaller than the hole-like γ pocket near the M point. Meanwhile, in bulk La 4Ni 3O10 , the sizes of these two pockets are more comparable, indicating improved FS nesting. Better size matching enhances the nesting and thus Tc [49]. Overall, the reduced superconducting Tc in the hybrid-phase system can be attributed to the weak-ened nesting condition in the TL subsystem compared to bulk La 4Ni 3O10 . Notably, recent ARPES measurements on the 1313 film reveal the disappearance of the γ pocket, which eliminates the nesting between the ε and γ pockets and thus strongly suppresses superconductivity [35, 41]. On the other hand, in quasi-2D layered superconduc-tors such as 1313 La 3Ni 2O7, the establishment of bulk SC requires not only intra-trilayer pairing but also inter-trilayer phase coherence. Given that the intermediate SL subsystem is close to a Mott-insulating state, it can be effectively regarded as a spacer layer whose itiner-ant electrons contribute negligibly to superconductivity. Physically, the inter-trilayer phase coherence is estab-6lished via the coherent tunneling of a Cooper pair from one superconducting TL subsystem to an adjacent one— namely, the interlayer Josephson coupling (IJC)—across the intervening normal-metallic SL subsystems, as shown in Fig. 5(c). Microscopically, this process originates from virtual single-particle tunneling events: a Cooper pair transfer involves two successive single-electron hop-pings between adjacent TLs, making the IJC second or-der in the inter-trilayer single-particle tunneling ampli-tude. Furthermore, this process is extremely weak in 1313 La 3Ni 2O7, as reflected by the very small effective TL–TL tunneling tT L z ∼ 10 −4 listed in Tab. II. Conclusion. We present a comprehensive investi-gation of the electronic properties and superconduct-ing mechanism in the hybrid 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 system, which consists of alternating SL and TL subsystems. Based on DFT+DMFT calculations, we find that the SL subsystem behaves as a nearly insulating bad metal, with the dz2 orbital exhibiting Mott behavior and the 

dx2−y2 orbital lacking well-defined quasiparticle excita-tion, whereas the TL subsystem displays layer-dependent correlations. Therefore, SC primarily resides within the TL subsystem, which is found to be hole-doped with a reduction in Ni-eg occupancy relative to bulk La 4Ni 3O10 .To further elucidate the low-energy physics, we construct an effective model for the TL subsystem that incorpo-rates electronic correlation effects, using the insights from DFT+DMFT. Using the renormalized Hamiltonian, our RPA analysis reveals that SC in 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 exhibits an s±-wave pairing symmetry similar to that of bulk La 4Ni 3O10 . Due to the presence of slight hole doping and the weakened interlayer Josephson coupling, the su-perconducting Tc of 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 is significantly lower than that of bulk La 4Ni 3O10 and markedly lower than that of 2222 La 3Ni 2O7. Our results demonstrate that the SL subsystem is detrimental to high-Tc SC in 1313 La 3Ni 2O7, both electronically (through Mott physics) and functionally (by weakening Josephson coupling and global phase coherence). This stands in stark contrast to the 2222 La 3Ni 2O7, which supports robust pairing inter-actions and strong interlayer coherence. Consistent with our theoretical results, recent experiments hves shown that the 2222 La 3Ni 2O7 thin films exhibit SC, whereas the 1313 La 3Ni 2O7 thin films do not [35, 41]. These experimental observations further corroborate that the genuine high-Tc phase in La 3Ni 2O7 family is the 2222 bi-layer structure, rather than the 1313 phase. Our work establishes design rules for achieving enhanced phase co-herence and high-Tc in hybrid nickelate superconductors and other layered transition-metal oxides.
