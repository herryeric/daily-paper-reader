## 摘要
交变磁体 (AMs) 以零净磁化强度和动量相关的自旋分裂为特征，预计在产生多种奇异且罕见的超导态方面具有巨大潜力。然而，迄今为止尚未在交变磁体中实现超导性。最近，二维 (2D) V$_2$Ch$_2$O (Ch = Se, Te) 单层以及包含 [V$_2$Ch$_2$O]$^{δ-}$ 结构层的 AV$_2$Ch$_2$O (A = K, Rb, Cs) 晶体已被预测和/或证明是极具前景的交变磁性材料。我们初步尝试通过施加压力或化学掺杂在这些材料中探索超导性，但未获成功。在此，我们报道了在一种新合成的层状化合物 Na$_{2-x}$V$_2$Se$_2$O（AV$_2$Ch$_2$O 的一种变体）中发现了转变温度约为 16.3 K 的超导性。在该结构中，[V$_2$Ch$_2$O]$^{δ-}$ 层之间穿插着 Na$^+$ 双层，而非单层 A$^+$，且钠位点仅为半填充。这一类缺乏反演对称性的新型层状钒氧硫族化物为探索交变磁性超导体提供了一个有趣的平台，并具有揭示拓扑态、范霍夫奇异性和有限动量超导等新颖现象的潜力。此外，该材料充当了铜氧化物/镍氧化物与铁基高温超导体之间的“桥梁”，为扩展具有更高临界温度 (T$_c$) 的层状超导体类别提供了新的希望和机遇，并增强了我们对这些系统中潜在机制的理解。

## Abstract
Altermagnets (AMs), characterized by zero net magnetization and momentum-dependent spin splitting, are anticipated to hold significant potential for generating multiple exotic and uncommon superconducting states. However, superconductivity has not yet been realized in AMs to date. Recently, two-dimensional (2D) V$_2$Ch$_2$O (Ch = Se, Te) monolayers, as well as AV$_2$Ch$_2$O (A = K, Rb, Cs) crystals containing [V$_2$Ch$_2$O]$^{δ-}$ building layers, have been predicted and/or demonstrated to be promising altermagnetic materials. Our preliminary attempts to explore superconductivity in these materials by applying pressure or chemical doping were unsuccessful. Here we report the discovery of superconductivity at a relatively high transition temperature of ~ 16.3 K in a newly synthesized layered compound, Na$_{2-x}$V$_2$Se$_2$O, a variant of AV$_2$Ch$_2$O. In this structure, the [V$_2$Ch$_2$O]$^{δ-}$ layers are interspersed with double layers of Na$^+$ instead of a single layer of A$^+$, with sodium sites being only half-filled. This new family of layered vanadium oxychalcogenides, lacking inversion symmetry, represents an intriguing platform for exploring altermagnetic superconductors, and holds the potential to reveal novel phenomena, such as topological states, van Hove singularities, and finite-momentum superconductivity. Furthermore, this material acts as a "bridge" between the cuprate/nickelate and iron-pnictide high temperature superconductors, providing new hope and opportunity to expand the category of layered superconductors with higher critical temperatures (T$_c$) and enhancing our understanding of the underlying mechanisms in these systems.
