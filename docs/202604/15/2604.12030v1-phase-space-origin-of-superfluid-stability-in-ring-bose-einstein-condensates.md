## 摘要
我们基于 Wigner 相空间形式，提出了环形玻色-爱因斯坦凝聚体中超流电流的动力学描述。从环面几何中的 Gross-Pitaevskii 方程出发，我们推导出了角 Wigner 函数的 Vlasov 型方程，其中平均场相互作用产生了一个与密度梯度成正比的有效力。在该框架内，我们获得了集体模式的色散关系，并在长波极限下恢复了 Bogoliubov 谱。我们表明，超流性的 Landau 判据可以解释为不存在满足条件 \(ω= q v_\ell\) 的共振相空间轨迹。在环形几何中，角动量的量子化导致了一组离散的速度，这抑制了共振态的可用性，并强烈抑制了 Landau 阻尼。相比之下，在连续极限 \(R \to \infty\) 下，能谱变为准连续的，并恢复了标准的 Landau 阻尼机制，从而在动力学共振与超流性的能量判据之间建立了直接联系。我们通过考虑有限宽度的角动量分布，进一步分析了 Bogoliubov 耗尽的作用。尽管在这种情况下形式上存在共振态，但我们表明，对于低于声速的流速，相空间分布不提供能量转移所需的梯度，超流电流保持动力学稳定。我们的结果提供了超流性的统一相空间解释，强调了角动量量子化和分布函数结构在决定持久电流稳定性中的作用。

## Abstract
We present a kinetic description of superfluid currents in ring-shaped Bose-Einstein condensates based on the Wigner phase-space formalism. Starting from the Gross-Pitaevskii equation in a toroidal geometry, we derive a Vlasov-type equation for the angular Wigner function, in which the mean-field interaction generates an effective force proportional to the density gradient. Within this framework, we obtain the dispersion relation of collective modes and recover the Bogoliubov spectrum in the long-wavelength limit. We show that the Landau criterion for superfluidity can be interpreted as the absence of resonant phase-space trajectories satisfying the condition \(ω= q v_\ell\). In a ring geometry, the quantization of angular momentum leads to a discrete set of velocities, which suppresses the availability of resonant states and strongly inhibits Landau damping. In contrast, in the continuous limit \(R \to \infty\), the spectrum becomes quasi-continuous and the standard Landau damping mechanism is recovered, establishing a direct connection between kinetic resonances and the energetic criterion for superfluidity. We further analyze the role of Bogoliubov depletion by considering a finite-width angular momentum distribution. Although resonant states formally exist in this case, we show that, for flow velocities below the sound velocity, the phase-space distribution does not provide the gradients required for energy transfer, and the superfluid current remains dynamically stable. Our results provide a unified phase-space interpretation of superfluidity, highlighting the role of angular momentum quantization and the structure of the distribution function in determining the stability of persistent currents.
