## Topological anisotropic non-Fermi liquid from a Berry-dipole semimetal 

Konstantinos Ladovrechis 

Theoretische Physik III, Ruhr-Universität Bochum, D-44801 Bochum, Germany 

(Dated: April 16, 2026) Investigating the interplay among topology and electron-electron interactions is an intriguing re-search quest which has recently gathered steam across the community of condensed-matter physics. In the present work, we study the fate of a three-dimensional Berry-dipole semimetal, lying at the topological quantum critical point separating a Hopf insulator from a trivial insulator, in the presence of long-range Coulomb interactions. Utilizing large-Nf analysis at three spatial dimen-sions and an ϵ-expansion within the renormalization-group scheme, we uncover the emergence of a spatially anisotropic non-Fermi liquid with enhanced Berry-dipole moment. We further derive the corresponding scaling relations of certain physical observables as functions of the probed energy and temperature scale, and we provide a simple observational criterion for distinguishing the onset of the topological anisotropic non-Fermi liquid from a Berry-dipole semimetal. 

Introduction . The concepts of topology and quantum criticality have been fundamental research disciplines for condensed-matter physics in the last three decades [1–4]. In the presence of a finite Fermi surface, they might ap-pear as synergistic or competing constituents as far as the full spectrum and response functions are concerned. When interactions can be ignored, the single-particle for-malism is sufficient for determining the latter, thus a com-plete classification for the corresponding phases of matter can be constructed [5–7]. However, generically, interac-tions cannot be ignored and in that case a systematic categorization is far from being settled. In particular, long-range Coulomb interactions in topological semimet-als lead to a vanishing quasi-particle residue and to the invalidation of a single-particle representation. The result is the emergence of many-particle non-Fermi liquids, hav-ing been proposed to exist in Luttinger semimetals (see Ref. [8] for the features of delicate topology), Dirac, and Weyl semimetals [9–13] as well as at topological quan-tum critical points (TQCPs) distinguising double- and triple-Weyl semimetals from a trivial insulator [14, 15]. A natural extension towards utilizing correlations to gen-erate novel phases of matter with potentially underlying topology is the investigation of the effect of long-range Coulomb interaction on the TQCP separating the time-reversal-breaking topological phase of matter called Hopf insulator from a trivial insulator [16, 17]. In the low-energy regime, such a TQCP is described in terms of a Berry-dipole semimetal , which is a three-dimensional fermionic system with a quadratic band-touching at the origin of the Brillouin zone supporting a finite Berry-dipole moment [18, 19]. The question for the existence of novel phases of matter as derivatives of a Berry-dipole semimetal becomes even more relevant due to the recent experimental realization of the latter in acoustic lattices [20], on one hand, and of the Hopf insulators in nitrogen-vacancy centers and topoelectric circuits [21, 22], on the other hand. In this Letter, we show that a derivative phase of mat-ter emerging from the Berry-dipole semimetal exhibits 

anisotropic non-Fermi-liquid behavior. The vanishing of the density of states in the vicinity of the origin of the Brillouin zone causes the long-range Coulomb interaction to be a relevant perturbation from a renormalization-group point of view [23, 24]. The considered Berry-dipole semimetal manifests a four-fold rotational symme-try around the ˆz axis, which indicates potential differen-tiation in the scaling of the form factors under Coulomb interactions. Within a large-Nf analysis corroborated by an ϵ-expansion through the renormalization-group scheme, we indeed find that the interaction-induced semimetal shows strong spatial anisotropy resulting in scaling relations for physical observables significantly dif-ferent from the Fermi-liquid estimates [25]. Finally, the resulting enhancement of the fluxes of Berry curvature through the upper- and lower-half z-planes enables us to provide an observational test for the onset of the topolog-ical anisotropic non-Fermi-liquid behavior in terms of the instrinsic and extrinsinc non-linear Hall conductivities.

Hence, the fate of the flux of Berry curvature is manifested in the non-linear Hall response leading to the following observational criterion: as a function of the long-range Coulomb inter-action, the Berry-dipole semimetal undergoes a quantum phase transition into a topological anisotropic non-Fermi liquid when both the intrinsic and extrinsic non-linear Hall conductivities show giant enhancement. 

Conclusion . In summary, we studied the fate of the three-dimensional Berry-dipole semimetal in the presence of long-range Coulomb interactions. Using a large-Nf

analysis and an ϵ-expansion within the renormalization-group scheme, we uncovered a novel semimetallic phase of matter featuring spatially anisotropic non-Fermi liq-uid behavior with an enhanced topology in terms of large fluxes of Berry curvature in the upper- and lower-half z-planes. Such a non-Fermi liquid behavior can be experi-mentally probed in acoustic polar lattices, and potentially in nitrogen-vacancy centers and topoelectric circuits, by measuring the power-law corrections to the density of states, compressibility, diamagnetic and optical conduc-tivities as well as the diverging behavior of the intrinsic and extrinsic non-linear Hall conductivities.
