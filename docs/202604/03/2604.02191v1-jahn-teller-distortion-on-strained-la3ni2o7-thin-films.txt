(Dated: April 3, 2026) We present a systematic study of the electronic structure of strained La 3Ni 2O7 thin films. We show that biaxial compressive strain mainly elongates the outer apical Ni-O bond while leaving the inner apical Ni-O bond nearly unchanged. As a result, the Jahn–Teller splitting ∆JT is strongly enhanced, whereas the interlayer dz2 hopping tz

> ⊥

changes only weakly. Since superconductivity is widely believed to emerge only below a critical in-plane lattice constant, our results identify the strain-enhanced ∆JT as the relevant microscopic tuning parameter. Consistently, the calculated Fermi surfaces and Hall response for LaAlO 3 and SrLaAlO 4 substrates agree with ARPES and Hall measurements. Our results identify Jahn–Teller distortion as a key tuning parameter in strained La 3Ni 2O7

and support its central role in optimizing superconductivity in bilayer nickelates. 

The recent discovery of superconductivity in the bilayer nickelate La 3Ni 2O7 under high pressure has sparked renewed interest in layered nickelates as candidates for unconven-tional superconductivity [1–6]. Unlike infinite-layer nicke-late and cuprate superconductors, La 3Ni 2O7 belongs to the Ruddlesden–Popper n = 2 series and features closely spaced NiO 2 bilayers [5], as illustrated in Fig. 1(a). Under applied pressure, superconductivity emerges with a high transition temperature [1, 3, 7–20], indicating that structural and orbital degrees of freedom beyond those of single-layer systems play a central role. Identifying the essential ingredients responsi-ble for superconductivity in this material therefore remains an outstanding open problem. A central issue in this context concerns the role of the Ni 

d3z2−r2 (dz2 ) orbital. In contrast to cuprates, La 3Ni 2O7 exhibits pronounced multi-orbital character, with the relevant atomic energy levels summarized in Fig. 1(b) [1, 5]. In particular, the 

dz2 orbital participates in strong interlayer coupling tz

> ⊥

> z2

> z2

) molec-ular orbitals across the bilayer. This interlayer hybridization has been widely proposed as a key factor in stabilizing super-conductivity [21–51]. At the same time, the importance of the Jahn–Teller (JT) splitting energy ∆JT between the dx2−y2

and dz2 orbitals has often been underestimated [5]. How the competition between interlayer coupling tz 

> ⊥

and JT splitting 

∆JT shapes the electronic structure of La 3Ni 2O7 remains un-resolved. The recent realization of La 3Ni 2O7 thin film superconduc-tivity under substrate-induced strain at ambient pressure pro-vides a new platform to address this issue [2, 4, 52–66]. Ex-perimentally, the crystal structures associated with supercon-ducting La 3Ni 2O7 are summarized in Fig. 1(c,d) [1, 2, 12, 61]. It has been proposed that superconductivity appears only when the in-plane lattice constant ap is reduced below a crit-ical value [2], as shown in Fig. 1(c). In contrast, thin-film studies indicate that the out-of-plane lattice constant c is not essential for the emergence of superconductivity [55, 60, 61], as shown in Fig. 1(d). Our results demonstrate that JT physics plays a key role in shaping the electronic structure in La 3Ni 2O7.More precisely, the essential structural motif of La 3Ni 2O7 is the network of corner-sharing NiO 6 octahedra forming closely spaced bilayers, as illustrated in Fig. 1(a). These bilayer octa-hedra arrange into a square lattice within the ab plane, giving rise to the quasi-two-dimensional crystal structure. In a naive local picture, one may assume that each Ni ion resides in an

However, the bilayer geometry in-troduces two crucial modifications beyond this simple picture: (i) inequivalent apical oxygen ions and JT distortions , and (ii) 

strong interlayer coupling between the d z2 orbitals .From a symmetry perspective, the inner apical oxygen O m

This symmetry opera-tion relates the top and bottom Ni sites within the bilayer. The total apical height along the c direction is then defined as h = zt + zm, as shown in Fig. 1(a). This result is directly relevant to experiments. Supercon-ductivity in La 2PrNi 2O7 thin films has been realized on SLAO (Tc,0 ≈ 30 K) and LAO ( Tc,0 ≈ 3 K) substrates [54, 61]. Previ-ous studies have suggested that the LAO lattice constant lies near the critical strain condition for superconductivity [61], as summarized in Fig. 1(c,d). Remarkably, this relatively small lattice change is accompanied by an order-of-magnitude vari-ation in Tc. Combined with the strong ap-dependence of ∆JT 

obtained above, this observation indicates that the enhance-ment of superconductivity is closely tied to the strain-induced increase of the JT splitting. To further elucidate the role of JT distortion, we compare electronic structures for SLAO and LAO substrates by cal-culating their Fermi surfaces, shown in Fig. 3(a,b), with TB parameters specified in Appendix B. For SLAO, we obtain two Fermi surfaces: an electron-like α-pocket and a hole-like β-pocket, whose shapes agree well with recent ARPES measurements [57]. In contrast, for LAO, an additional hole-like γ-pocket appears near the Brillouin zone corner, as high-lighted in Fig. 3(b). As the JT distortion increases, the onsite energy of dz2 orbitals is lowered relative to the dx2−y2 orbital, as shown in Fig. 3(d). Consequently, the γ-band, which is pre-4dominantly of symmetric d+ 

> z2

character near the zone corner, is pushed below the Fermi level, resulting in the disappearance of the γ-pocket. This establishes a ∆JT -driven Lifshitz tran-sition associated with the appearance or disappearance of the 

γ-pocket. The di ff erence in the Fermi surfaces has immediate con-sequences in the Hall coe ffi cients. Using the obtained band structures and a uniform quasiparticle scattering rate Γ, we calculate the Hall coe ffi cient RH = σxy /σ xx σyy [74] (see Ap-pendix D for details), with results shown as solid lines in Fig. 3(c). For both substrates, we find RH is negative, but it is significantly more negative for SLAO. This reflects the carrier compensation is controlled by the JT distortion. Specifically, as ∆JT increases, the hole-like γ-pocket disappears, and the contribution from the β-pocket is further suppressed due to the changes in the Fermi surface area, while contributions from the electron-like α-pocket remain largely unchanged. The re-sults calculated show qualitative agreement with experimental data [61], plotted in dashed lines, where the magnitude of RH

for LAO is about 4-5 times smaller than that of SLAO. This further shows that ∆JT controls the balance between di ff erent charge carriers through shaping the band structures. Lastly, we examine the implications of JT distortion for superconductivity through its e ff ect on orbital character. As shown in Fig. 3(d), the local electronic structure evolves with increasing JT distortion, with the anti-symmetric d− 

> z2

∆JT and tz

> z2

> x2−y2

> z2

> z2

Combined with the experimental observation that su-perconductivity is strongly enhanced from LAO to SLAO, this supports a picture in which JT distortion promotes supercon-ductivity by balancing the orbital contributions and stabilizing the molecular Mott regime favorable for pairing. In summary, our calculations and analyses show that epi-taxial strain in La 3Ni 2O7 thin films primarily enhances the JT splitting ∆JT , while the interlayer hopping tz 

> ⊥

changes only weakly. This behavior originates from the asymmetric re-sponse of the apical Ni–O bonds: the outer bond is strongly elongated by in-plane compression, whereas the inner bond remains nearly unchanged. The resulting increase of ∆JT con-trols the low-energy electronic structure and accounts for the substrate dependence of the Fermi surface and Hall response. In particular, the calculated results for LAO and SLAO are consistent with ARPES and Hall measurements. These results identify JT distortion as the leading electronic e ff ect of epitax-ial strain and a key tuning parameter for superconductivity in strained La 3Ni 2O7 and related bilayer nickelates. 5
