I. INTRODUCTION 

It was suggested that the statistical structure of the Kolmogorov turbulent cascade can be described by non-additive entropy 1. Here we consider the Vinen turbulence 2,3 , which is characterized by a single scale – the distance l

between the vortex lines. Nevertheless, we show that the Vinen turbulence has also the signatures of the non-additive entropy. This can be seen by comparison of the Vinen turbulence with the black hole thermodynamics, which has the non-additive configurational entropy. The black hole entropy can be obtained by consideration of the probability of splitting of a black hole into two or more black holes in the processes of macroscopic quantum tunneling. Such process can be considered as thermodynamic fluctuation, and according to Landau and Lifshitz 4 its rate is determined by the difference in entropy before and after the splitting. From this difference one obtains 5 that the black hole entropy is proportional the horizon area and obeys the Tsallis-Cirto statistics 6 with δ = 2. The process of the creation of quantized vortex rings in a moving superfluid is also described by the phenomenon of macroscopic quantum tunneling 7. This allows us to estimate the entropy of the Vinen turbulence. It appears that the vortex line ensemble in the Vinen quantum turbulence in superfluids is described by the non-extensive Tsallis-Cirto statistics with δ = 3. This also determines the temperature of the Vinen turbulence and the first law of thermodynamics. 

II. ENTROPY OF VINEN TURBULENCE 

Let us consider the possible entropy of the Vinen turbulence. As distinct from the Kolmogorov turbulence, the Vinen turbulence 2,3 is characterized by a single length scale l (the inter-vortex distance or the length of vortex loop), which is determined by the velocity v of the flow. We assume that the entropy is related to the entropy characterizing the quantum tunneling formation of vortex rings.

> V. FIRST LAW OF VINEN TURBULENCE

One can also consider the thermodynamics in the elementary cell of Vinen turbulence. In this cell of the volume 

V0 ∝ l3, the energy and entropy scale as E ∝ l and S ∝ l3 correspondingly. Since the temperature T ∝ v2 ∝ l−2, the Vinen turbulence scaling law is consistent with the first law of thermodynamics, dE = T dS .This first law can be modified by adding the pair of thermodynamic variables, that characterize the Vinen turbulence: the velocity v and its thermodynamic conjugate variable, which is proportional the area A of the vortex ring. These thermodynamic variables follow from the canonically conjugate variables describing the dynamics of the vortex ring 7:the coordinate z along the normal to the vortex ring and the momentum of the vortex ring, pz = 2 πℏnA . Since the velocity v = dz/dt , this gives rise to the thermodynamic pair, v and 2 πℏnA . This pair must also contribute to the first law of Vinen thermodynamics. The variables v and A look similar to the pair of the thermodynamic conjugate variables in the black hole thermo-dynamics – the gravitational coupling K = 116 πG and the area A of the black hole horizon 5. 

> VI. CONNECTION TO DE SITTER THERMODYNAMICS

The thermodynamic pair, v and 2 πℏnA , has also analogy with the pair that enters the thermodynamics of the de Sitter Universe 11 : the gravitational coupling K and the Riemann curvature R. Emergence of the thermodynamic variable R in de Sitter is an example of the so-called Kronecker anomaly 12 , or of the Larkin-Pikin effect 13 . The flow velocity v as a thermodynamic variable is another example of the Kronecker anomaly. There is another connection between the entropy of Vinen turbulence and the de Sitter entropy. The entropy of de Sitter state S(V ) is extensive: it is proportional to the volume V of the considered part of the de Sitter vacuum. On the other hand the entropy S(VH ) of the Hubble volume VH, which corresponds to the entropy of the cosmological horizon, is non-extensive. It is proportional to the area A of the cosmological horizon and obeys the Tsallis-Cirto statistics. The same takes place for the entropy of the Vinen turbulence, where the role of the horizon radius 1 /H is played by the scale R0 ∼ l of the Vinen turbulence. The entropy S(l) is non-extensive and obeys the Tsallis-Cirto statistics in Eq.(4). On the other hand the entropy of the part of the turbulent superfluid with volume V ≫ V0 is extensive, being proportional to the volume, S(V ) ∼ nV , where n is the density of atoms.  

> VII. CONCLUSION

We consider the analogy between thermodynamics of the Vinen turbulence and thermodynamics of the cosmological objects: black holes and de Sitter Universe. The analogy with black hole is based on the phenomenon of macroscopic quantum tunnelling, that regulates the process of quantum mechanical formation of quantized vortices in superfluids and processes of splitting of the black holes into smaller parts. The rate of both processes is determined by the change in the entropy of the system, which allows us to find the black hole entropy, which is proportional to the area A of the black hole horizon, and to estimate the entropy of the Vinen turbulence. From the entropy of the ensemble of black holes and the entropy of the ensemble of vortex lines we obtain the corresponding temperature: the Hawking temperature of the black hole and the temperature of the Vinen turbulence. The latter is proportional to the kinetic energy of the flow in Eq.(9), which also corresponds to the energy density of the vortex lines in Eq.(10). The analogy with the thermodynamics of de Sitter Universe provides additional elements to the consideration of the entropy of Vinen turbulence. As distinct from the black hole, the de Sitter state is homogeneous. That is why its entropy is extensive being equal to the entropy density multiplied by the volume V under consideration. On the other hand the entropy of the Hubble volume VH is proportional to the area of the cosmological horizon, SH = A/ 4G, and this entropy is non-extensive. 4A similar duality between extensive and non-extensive behaviour of entropy also exists for Vinen turbulence, where the role of the Hubble radius is played the Vinen scale l. The non-extensive side of the entropy of the Vinen ensemble is determined by the composition law given in equation (4). It corresponds to the Tsallis-Csirto statistics with δ = 3.
