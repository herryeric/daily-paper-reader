## Thermodynamic Identification of the Internal Superconducting Phase Boundary in UTe 2 for H ‖ b

Michal Valiˇ ska, ∗ Tetiana Haidamak, Andrej Cabala, Petr Proschek, and Vladim´ ır Sechovsk´ y

(Dated: April 29, 2026) The H–T phase diagram of UTe 2 for magnetic field along the hard b axis contains an unresolved internal boundary near μ0H ∼ 14–15 T, previously inferred from ac susceptibility and transport experiments but lacking thermodynamic evidence. We report ultrasound results for several elastic modes in an ultraclean UTe 2 single crystal with Tc > 2 K for H ‖ b down to 0.33 K and up to 18 T. A pronounced anomaly in the longitudinal C33 mode, with a weaker response in C44 and no resolvable anomaly in C55 , establishes this feature as a bulk thermodynamic phase boundary and reveals a symmetry-selective coupling to lattice strain. The phase line remains nearly constant in field near 14 T and terminates near 13.5 T and 1.25 K at a tetracritical point, providing the thermo-dynamic evidence for the fourth phase boundary in the H–T phase diagram. The results constrain the order-parameter structure of the high-field phase and support field-induced multicomponent superconductivity in UTe 2.

Since the discovery of superconductivity in UTe 2 by Ran et al. , this heavy-fermion paramagnet has become one of the central platforms for the study of spin-triplet and potentially topological superconductivity [1]. Early thermodynamic and transport studies rapidly estab-lished UTe 2 as an unconventional superconductor with a large and highly anisotropic upper critical field, strong-coupling behavior, and a striking sensitivity to magnetic field, pressure, and sample quality [2–4]. High-field and high-pressure measurements subsequently revealed a re-markably rich superconducting phase diagram whose de-tailed topology remains intensely debated [5–12]. The interpretation of UTe 2 is strongly affected by sample quality. Molten-salt-flux growth and related liquid-transport methods enabled ultraclean crystals with Tc > 2 K [13–15], while uranium deficiency and uranium-site defects were identified as key sources of suppressed or split transitions [16, 17]. The same ul-traclean generation of crystals also enabled quantum-oscillation and quantum-interference studies of the quasi-two-dimensional Fermi surface [18–20], and revealed the enhancement of superconductivity in intense magnetic fields [21]. This issue is crucial for the high-field phase diagram for H ‖ b, where ultraclean crystals revealed additional anomalies near μ0H ∼ 14–15 T in ac suscepti-bility and transport studies [11, 22, 23]. These anomalies were interpreted as an internal superconducting phase boundary, but a bulk thermodynamic signature has re-mained missing. A multicomponent spin-triplet scenario has further predicted a nearly horizontal internal line ter-minating at a tetracritical point near 15 T [24]. This question has acquired additional interest through recent progress on the p–T phase diagram. In the broader 

H–p–T phase space, two locations have emerged where only three second-order phase boundaries appeared to meet, implying an incomplete and therefore thermo-dynamically problematic topology unless an additional phase line is present. One of these cases, in the p–T

plane, has now been resolved by recent ultrasound mea-surements that uncovered the missing thermodynamic boundary and established a tetracritical point (TetCP) [25]. At the same time, the high-field and high-pressure superconducting phases were shown to be continuously connected [12], and the corresponding H–p–T construc-tion implies that the pressure-induced TetCP evolves into a tetracritical line extending toward ambient pressure and the high-field superconducting regime [25]. Within this picture, the long-suspected anomaly near 14–15 T for H ‖ b is naturally expected to represent the miss-ing internal phase boundary whose endpoint at ambient pressure forms the high-field TetCP. Here we address this issue using pulse-echo ultrasound measurements of the elastic constants Cij on an ultr-aclean UTe 2 crystal with Tc > 2 K for magnetic field applied along the b axis. We resolve a distinct anomaly in C33 (H) within the superconducting state that traces a nearly temperature-independent line near 14 T between 0.33 and 1.1 K. We show that this anomaly constitutes bulk thermodynamic evidence for the fourth phase line in the H–T phase diagram. The line terminates near 13.5 T and 1.25 K at a TetCP, consistent with the tetracritical-line scenario recently established in the p–T phase dia-gram [25]. Our results therefore place the high-field H–T

phase diagram of UTe 2 on firm thermodynamic footing and provide further support for multicomponent super-conductivity in the ultraclean limit. The phase boundary sought in this work is exam-ined through its coupling to lattice strain εij . Ultra-

Figure 1(a) displays the field dependence of the lon-gitudinal mode C33 at selected temperatures. A clear softening anomaly, marked by arrows, is resolved in-side the superconducting state at a nearly temperature-independent field of about 14 T. Since C33 is a bulk elastic constant, this anomaly establishes the missing thermodynamic signature of the internal high-field phase boundary previously inferred only from ac susceptibility and transport measurements [11, 22, 23]. The anomaly is visible from the lowest measured temperature up to 1.1 K, and its amplitude increases substantially on ap-proaching the multicritical region where the line termi-nates at the TetCP. At higher temperatures, the feature weakens and shifts to lower fields as the system moves away from the tetracritical regime. For the 0.9 and 1.1 K isotherms, an additional kink is observed at higher field, consistent with the transition into the upper high-field superconducting phase [6, 9, 21]. The enhancement of the C33 anomaly on approach-ing the TetCP is consistent with the known sensitivity of elastic constants to critical and multicritical fluctuations in correlated uranium systems, including metamagnetic critical endpoints in UTe 2 and UCoAl [30, 31] and tri-critical fluctuations in UIrSi 3 [32]. Although a TetCP is distinct from a conventional critical endpoint, the same enhanced lattice response appears to be operative here. A weaker anomaly is also observed in the transverse mode C44 [Fig. 1(b)]. It has the opposite sign, appearing as a hardening near 14 T, but its field position tracks the 

C33 anomaly, indicating that both modes detect the same thermodynamic boundary. By contrast, no correspond-ing anomaly is resolved in C55 within our experimental resolution [Fig. 1(c)]. This mode selectivity mirrors the pressure-induced tetracritical case, where the newly iden-tified internal boundary under pressure was prominent in the C33 elastic response but not comparably visible in C55 

[25]. The combination of strong C33 , weaker but finite 

C44 , and absent C55 response shows that the high-field phase beyond the internal boundary couples anisotropi-cally to lattice strain and provides a symmetry-sensitive constraint on its order-parameter structure. The inset of Fig. 1(a) shows the corresponding trans-verse magnetostriction ∆ lc/l c for H ‖ b. A kink is ob-served at essentially the same field as the anomaly in 

C33 , confirming that the field-induced boundary affects not only the dynamic elastic stiffness but also the static equilibrium lattice distortion. Because both elastic con-stants and magnetostriction derive from the same strain-dependent free energy, the anomaly cannot be attributed merely to vortex dynamics or to a transport-specific fea-ture. 3

The marked anomalies pro-vide complementary transition points for constructing the phase diagram in Fig. 3.The mode selectivity also has symmetry implications. In orthorhombic D2h symmetry, different shear modes probe different strain channels: C44 couples to εbc , C55 

to εac , and C66 to εab [27, 28]. Recent ultrasound work argued that the low-field superconducting state is single-component, with an odd-parity B2u state as the most compatible candidate, based on the absence of shear-modulus discontinuities at Tc [28]. In this context, the finite anomaly in C44 , together with the absence of a resolvable C55 response, shows that the phase beyond the internal boundary is not a simple continuation of the low-field state but rather couples to strain in a symmetry-selective manner. The present data do not uniquely de-termine the secondary component, particularly without 

C66 , but they provide thermodynamic evidence that the high-field phase carries additional order-parameter struc-ture, consistent with multicomponent scenarios for the tetracritical region [24, 25, 33, 34]. The anomaly positions extracted from the thermody-namic data are summarized in Fig. 3. Following the no-tation of the pressure-dependent phase diagram [12, 25], we denote the low-field superconducting state as SC1 and the upper high-field state as SC2. The newly resolved, nearly field-constant internal boundary near 14 T sepa-rates SC1 from an intermediate region naturally identi-fied as SC1+SC2 and terminates near 13.5 T and 1.25 K at a tetracritical point (TetCP), where four phase bound-aries meet. Thus, the ultrasound anomaly supplies the missing thermodynamic phase line required to complete the H ‖ b phase-diagram topology and provides direct support for field-induced multicomponent superconduc-tivity. It is consistent with the tetracritical-line scenario advanced from the pressure-dependent phase diagram [12, 24, 25], and echoes the role of sound velocity in sens-ing tetracritical topology in UPt 3 [35]. The local geometry near the TetCP also helps explain why this boundary has been difficult to detect calori-metrically. The four phase boundaries have approximate slopes dH/dT ∼ − 15, −11, −0.44, and +30 T/K, with the discussed boundary being almost horizontal.
