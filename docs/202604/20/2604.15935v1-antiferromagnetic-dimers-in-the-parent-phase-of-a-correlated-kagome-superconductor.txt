Kagome metals are prone to charge-density wave (CDW), magnetic, and superconducting phases, with their flat electronic band conducive for correlated physics. In contrast to the weakly correlated 

AV3Sb 5 (A = K, Rb, Cs) kagome metals with a 2 × 2 CDW, CsCr 3 Sb 5 is a correlated metal with a flat band close to the Fermi level, and exhibits a 4 × 1 CDW intertwined with magnetic order. Under pressure, the intertwined orders are suppressed and give way to a dome of superconductivity that emerges from a non-Fermi liquid normal state. Here, we solve the crystal structure of the 4 × 1CDW state in CsCr 3 Sb 5, and show it consists of Cr dimers separated by Cr chains. First-principles calculations show the dominant exchange interaction is antiferromagnetic within the dimers, while the intra-chain and dimer-chain couplings are much weaker. The CDW transition of CsCr 3 Sb 5 is found to be more strongly first-order than those in AV3Sb 5, without significant soft phonons or diffuse scattering above the CDW transition temperature. These findings suggest that fluctuating antiferromagnetic dimers may play a major role in the electron pairing of superconducting CsCr 3 Sb 5.

INTRODUCTION 

A common feature of unconventional superconductiv-ity (SC) in correlated materials is proximity to mag-netic instabilities [ 1–3], which can be intertwined with a symmetry-breaking electronic order, leading to charge or structural modulations [ 4–6]. Upon appropriate tun-ing, magnetic or intertwined orders may be destabilized at a quantum critical point (QCP), where the prolifera-tion of corresponding fluctuations may mediate a dome of unconventional SC [ 7–9]. In this context, an understand-ing of the ordered phase provides critical information for fluctuations at the QCP, and the SC that they potentially drive. The kagome lattice hosts van Hove singularities and flat bands, which may lead to unconventional SC and charge-density waves (CDWs) [ 10 –14 ]. The discovery of SC coexisting with a CDW in the AV3Sb 5 (A = K, Rb, Cs) kagome metals [ 15 , 16 ] led to proposals of uncon-ventional pairing [ 17 –19 ], while there is also compelling evidence for conventional SC [ 20 –23 ]. Furthermore, the CDW in AV3Sb 5 collapses via first-order-like transitions under pressure, and SC persists well beyond the CDW phase boundary [ 24 –26 ], rather than forming a singu-lar dome anchored around a QCP. The nonmagnetic and weakly correlated nature of AV3Sb 5 [15 , 27 ], as well as the absence of QCPs in their pressure-temperature phase diagrams, differentiate these compounds from the paradigm of SC in strongly correlated metals [ 7–9]. Recently, a correlated kagome metal CsCr 3Sb 5

with the same high-temperature structure as AV3Sb 5

[Fig. 1(a)] was discovered. Compared to AV3Sb 5 that shows a 2 × 2 in-plane charge modulation without mag-netic order, CsCr 3Sb 5 exhibits a 4 × 1 in-plane charge modulation coupled with antiferromagnetic (AFM) or-der below TCDW = TN ≈ 55 K. Specific heat mea-surements reveal an enhanced Sommerfeld coefficient 

γ ≈ 100 mJ ·K−1·mol −1, indicative of significant elec-tronic correlations [ 28 ]. Angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (ARPES) measurements reveal a flat band slightly below the Fermi level without band folding across 

TCDW [29 –31 ], and resonant inelastic X-ray scattering un-covered robust dipersionless magnetic excitations above and below TCDW [31 ]. Scanning tunneling microscopy (STM) measurements found a 4 × √3 CDW coexistent with the 4 × 1 CDW on the surface of CsCr 3Sb 5 [32 –

34 ]. Under pressure, the coupled CDW and magnetic transitions split in temperature and are gradually sup-pressed, both disappearing around a putative QCP at 4 GPa, giving way to a dome of SC that emerges from a non-Fermi-liquid normal state [Fig. 1(b)] [ 28 ]. Such a pressure-temperature phase diagram of CsCr 3Sb 5 con-trasts with those of AV3Sb 5, where Fermi-liquid behav-ior is retained across a first-order collapse of the CDW,

AV3Sb 5, CsCr 3Sb 5 exhibits physical properties and a pressure-temperature phase diagram akin to correlated superconductors. A central unanswered question in CsCr 3Sb 5 is the na-ture of its intertwined charge and magnetic orders, whose dynamic variants may persist under pressure and ac-count for both SC and the non-Fermi-liquid normal state. Given the intertwined nature of charge and magnetic or-ders in CsCr 3Sb 5, investigations of its magnetic order requires knowledge of its CDW. Density functional the-ory (DFT) calculations enumerated collinear magnetic ground states, and found a 4 × 2 charge modulation to be lowest in energy, with the corresponding q = 0 AFM order categorized as altermagnetic [ 35 ]. The discrepancy between DFT (a 4 × 2 CDW state lowest in energy) [ 35 ]and experiments (a 4 × 1 CDW in the ground state) [ 28 ]is reflective of the correlated nature of CsCr 3Sb 5 and its frustrated energy landscape of competing phases. These results underscore the need for an experimental deter-mination of the CDW structure in CsCr 3Sb 5, which can then be used to model its magnetic ground state and magnetic interactions. Here, we experimentally determine the crystal struc-ture of CsCr 3Sb 5 in its CDW state. The 4 × 1 modula-tion arises from the formation of Cr dimers separated by Cr chains. The Cr-Cr bond length is ≈ 2.56 ˚ A for the dimers, whereas other Cr-Cr bonds average to ≈ 2.78 ˚ A. Based on the experimentally determined crystal struc-ture, we performed DFT calculations to determine the magnetic ground state and associated magnetic interac-tions, which reveal a q = 0 altermagnetic state with a dominant AFM coupling within the dimer. Our findings form the basis for understanding the physics of CsCr 3Sb 5,particularly intriguing of which is the connection between AFM dimers in the parent phase, and Cooper pairs in the superconducting phase.

First order CDW transition and phonon measurements 

An absence of thermal hysteresis in resistivity and magnetization across TCDW suggests the coupled CDW and magnetic transitions in CsCr 3Sb 5 to be either second order or weakly first order [ 28 ]. Using the elastic chan-nel of inelastic X-ray scattering, we probed the inten-sity of the CDW peak Q = (2 .25 , 1.75 , 0), which shows saturation within a few Kelvin of its onset [Fig. 3(a)], directly evidencing a first order transition. A negligi-ble thermal hysteresis is observed between warming and cooling, consistent with physical property measurements [28 ]. A step-like transition with a Gaussian distribution of transition temperatures gives an excellent fit of the measured CDW intensity, yielding TCDW ≈ 52 K and a width of 0 .8 K. A fit to I ∝ (TCDW − T )2β with a Gaussian distribution of transitions temperatures leads to β ≈ 0.03, where the small exponent also excludes the transition being second order. Temperature dependence of the normalized CDW peak intensities are compared between CsCr 3Sb 5 and AV3Sb 5

compounds in Fig. 3(b). The CDW transitions are first order in CsV 3Sb 5 and RbV 3Sb 5 , and becomes second order in KV 3Sb 5 [38 –40 ]. In terms of the discontinuity in the CDW peak intensity, the discontinuity weakens from CsV 3Sb 5 to RbV 3Sb 5, and disappears in KV 3Sb 5.In CsCr 3Sb 5, the CDW transition is sharper (with larger discontinuity) than the AV3Sb 5 compounds, which indi-cates the transition is more strongly first-order, although still weakly first order such that a significant thermal hysteresis is not observed. Recent IXS measurements indicate the diffuse scat-tering in AV3Sb 5 result from soft phonons [ 42 , 43 ], and the absence of diffuse scattering in CsCr 3Sb 5 suggests that compared to AV3Sb 5, similar soft phonons are ei-ther absent or less prominent in CsCr 3Sb 5, consistent with its more strongly first order CDW transition. An absence of prominent soft phonons is consistent with our IXS measurements, which only shows an acous-tic phonon mode along (2 + q, 2 − q, 0) at 70 K, without signatures of soft modes at qCDW [Fig. 3(c)]. Phonon measurements were also performed in other Brillouin zones, also without qCDW soft phonons, and the detected 

qCDW acoustic phonons are essentially independent of temperature [Fig. 3(d)]. Raw and additional IXS data are presented in Supplementary Note 1 and Supplemen-tary Figs. 1-3. A similar absence of soft phonons was also reported in CsCr 3Sb 5 with short-range CDW [ 44 ]. A divergent E2g elastoresistance above TCDW has been reported for CsCr 3Sb 5 [36 ], which suggests strong ne-matic fluctuations in the high-temperature phase. How-ever, such nematic fluctuations do not lead to anomalies of the in-plane transverse acoustic phonons with the same symmetry [Fig. 3(c) and (d)], as widely observed in the parent compounds of iron-based superconductors [ 45 –47 ]. This suggests a weak coupling between nematicity and the lattice in CsCr 3Sb 5, which occurs in the iron-based superconductors upon overdoping [ 48 ]. 

Antiferromagnetic dimers in an altermagnetic ground state 

Based on the results in Ref. [ 35 ], we performed a high-throughput search for all possible collinear in-plane AFM magnetic configurations compatible with a 4 × 1 CDW supercell with space group P bam , and then relaxed the crystal structures and computed their associated ener-gies. The resulting state with the lowest energy is shown in Fig. 4(a), which shows Cr-Cr dimers separated by Cr chains, in remarkable agreement with the experimen-tally determined CDW structure in Fig. 2(a). The cal-culated ground state CDW structure also exhibits Cs-Cs dimers [Supplementary Fig. 4], consistent with experi-mental findings [Fig. 2(b)]. Other 4 × 1 P bam CDW structures with higher energies, and their associated mag-netic structures, are presented in Supplementary Note 2 and Supplementary Fig. 5. As shown in Fig. 4, the dimers in the calculated CDW ground state are AFM, while the Cr chains are FM. To probe the magnetic interactions between Cr atoms, we examined how flipping certain spins affects the energy of the ground state magnetic configuration. By flipping all the spins on Cr dimers [Fig. 4(c)], we find that the energy is only increased by 11.4 meV/f.u. DISCUSSION AND CONCLUSION 

Our findings reveal that the CDW structure of CsCr 3Sb 5 is very different from those in AV3Sb 5,ScV 6Sn 6, and LuNb 6Sn 6. In AV3Sb 5, nonmagnetic V atoms on the kagome lattice form star of David or in-verse star-of-David structures that result in a 2 × 2 CDW in the ab -plane [ 16 ]. In ScV 6Sn 6 and LuNb 6Sn 6, the 3 × 3 × 3 CDWs are dominated by the motion of Sn-Sc(Lu)-Sn trimers along c-axis, rather than in-plane dis-tortions of the V or Nb kagome lattice [ 54 , 55 ]. The in-plane modulations of these CDW structures retain the sixfold rotational symmetries of their high temperature phases, which maybe broken by the stacking of distorted planes along the c-axis. On the other hand, the CDW structure of CsCr 3Sb 5 is inherently strongly anisotropic, with Cr-Cr dimers separated by Cr chains that run along the a-axis of the 4 × 1 cell [Fig. 2(a)], which in combina-tion with three possible twin domains [Fig. 1(c)], natu-rally account for its three-state Potts nematicity [ 36 ]. As the ideal kagome lattice with AFM nearest-neighbor interactions is highly frustrated, CDW forma-tion provides a natural way to relieve this mangetic frus-tration and facilitate long-range magnetic order. There-fore, the CDW of CsCr 3Sb 5 is likely driven by the spin Jahn-Teller effect, which saves magnetic energy at the cost of elastic energy. For comparison, the CDWs in 

AV3Sb 5 are driven by the nesting between van Hove singularities close to the Fermi level [ 56 –59 ] or electron-phonon coupling [ 42 , 43 , 60 –63 ], whereas the CDWs in ScV 6Sn 6 and LuNb 6Sn 6 are driven by electron-phonon coupling [ 64 –66 ], potentially also involving nesting effects [67 ]. Under pressure, the intertwined CDW and magnetic transitions of CsCr 3Sb 5 split and are continuously sup-pressed [ 28 ], similar to magnetic and nematic orders in 7the iron-based superconductors [ 3, 5, 6]. Our findings show that the AFM coupling within Cr-Cr dimers is the dominant energy scale in the CDW state of CsCr 3Sb 5,with the associated fluctuations likely persisting beyond the the suppression of the CDW, and contribute to the non-Fermi-liquid state from which a dome of SC emerges. Resonant inelastic X-ray scattering detected two spin ex-citation modes in CsCr 3Sb 5 [31 ], and the dispersionless nature of these modes suggest they are associated with local entities, for which the AFM dimers uncovered in this work are natural candidates. In such a scenario, the persistence of such local excitations above TCDW [31 ]suggests that dynamic variants of ordered dimers in the CDW state survive even when the CDW is destabilized. SC at the verge of dimer formation has been reported in IrTe 2 [68 , 69 ], AuTe 2 [70 , 71 ], and CuIr 2S4 [72 ]. How-ever, in these cases the dimers are nonmagnetic, the dimer state is suppressed via first-order-like quantum phase transitions, and the associated SC emerge from Fermi liquid normal states. CsCr 3Sb 5 is thus unique compared to these systems, as its dimers are magnetic with a strong AFM coupling, the intertwined CDW and magnetic orders are continuously suppressed towards a putative QCP, and the SC emerges from a non-Fermi-liquid normal state. While our DFT calculations suggest ordered AFM dimers that lead to an overall altermagnetic state, it takes very little energy to flip both spins of a given dimer. This raises the interesting possibility that the dimers are in fact spin singlets (rather than AFM-ordered), which could become mobile as the CDW is suppressed under pressure. Such a scenario is akin to the long-sought goal of obtaining a superconductor by doping a resonating va-lence bond state or a valence bond solid [ 73 –75 ], where mobile spin singlets become Cooper pairs. While this possibility is tantalizing, we note the superconducting coherence length of CsCr 3Sb 5 from upper critical field measurements is around 5 nm [ 28 ], much larger than the dimers observed in the parent phase. In summary, we showed that the CDW of the cor-related kagome metal CsCr 3Sb 5 is characterized by a 

P bam structure consisting of AFM Cr-Cr dimers and FM chains, explicitly breaking the sixfold rotation symmetry of the high temperature phase. The CDW transition in CsCr 3Sb 5 is found to be more strongly first order than its vanadium-based sister compounds, likely accounting for the absence of diffuse scattering or prominent soft phonons above TCDW . Constrained by the experimen-tally determined structure, we obtained the magnetic structure of CsCr 3Sb 5 via a systematic search of collinear magnetic phases, and showed the dominant magnetic in-teractions is AFM and within the Cr dimers. The CDW and magnetic structures determined in this work form the basis for further understanding CsCr 3Sb 5, in which kagome physics, unconventional superconductivity, and strong electronic correlations intersect.
