(Dated: March 13, 2026) Electron-phonon ( e-ph) interactions play a crucial role in determining many properties of mate-rials. In this context, the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger (SSH) model, where atomic motion modulates the electronic hopping, has gained significant attention due to its potential for strong electron pairing in relation to high-Tc superconductivity. Previous studies of the SSH models have addressed many aspects of this problem, but have focused heavily on either dilute or half-filled models with disper-sionless (Einstein) phonons. Here, we study the effects of dispersive optical phonons on the lightly doped one-dimensional optical Hubbard-SSH model using the density matrix renormalization group. We observe a significant enhancement in singlet binding driven by phonon dispersion; however, by calculating various correlation functions, we find that the enhanced binding does not translate to increased superconducting correlations but rather robust bond correlations in the studied parameter regime. Nevertheless, the significant impact of phonon dispersion on these correlations highlights the need to go beyond the Einstein phonon limit while modeling realistic quantum materials. 

Introduction — Electron-phonon ( e-ph) coupling is one of the most fundamental interactions in condensed mat-ter physics and plays a key role in conventional supercon-ductivity [1, 2]. Two widely studied e-ph models are the Holstein [3, 4] and Su-Schrieffer-Heeger (SSH) [5] mod-els. The SSH type of interaction, where atomic motion modulates the electronic hopping, has gained significant attention recently due to theory studies suggesting that it can support the formation of mobile (bi)polarons [6, 7] in the dilute limit and could lead to high-Tc supercon-ductivity [8]. Beyond dilute fillings, the interplay be-tween electron-electron ( e-e) and e-ph interactions can lead to several novel phases. For example, the half-filled two-dimensional (2D) Hubbard-SSH (HSSH) model has competing antiferromagentic (AFM) and bond ordered wave (BOW) phases [9–14]. SSH-like interactions can also drive novel charge-density-wave (CDW) [15, 16] and quantum spin liquid phases [17]. Most studies of SSH models have considered disper-sionless Einstein phonons. While this simplification is sometimes driven by the increased computational com-plexity associated with treating dispersive modes, it is also believed to be a good approximation for systems where the phonon bandwidth is small compared to the average frequency [18]. However, optical phonons can have a large bandwidth in real materials, often reaching a significant fraction of the average phonon energy [19– 22]. At the same time, studies of dispersive models in the dilute limit have found that comparable dispersions can alter model predictions [23–30]. For example, in the Hubbard-Holstein model [27, 28], a dispersive phonon branch with low-energy modes at the zone center signifi-cantly enhances the formation of nearest-neighbor bipo-larons, whereas low-energy dispersive modes at the zone boundary reduce the polaron’s effective mass in the bond-SSH model [29]. Light (bi)polarons have also been re-ported for the nondispersive bond HSSH model in the dilute electron density limit [6, 7]. Given these findings, it is natural to ask whether similar strong binding persists at higher carrier concentrations and how it is affected by the phonon dispersion. Here we investigate dispersive optical phonons in a one-dimensional (1D) doped HSSH model using density matrix renormalization group (DMRG). We primarily focus on the lightly hole-doped regime ( ⟨n⟩ = 1−ρ, ρ ≪ 1)relevant to many transition metal oxides. (For example, our results are directly applicable to the recently syn-thesized 1D cuprate chain compounds [31].) As with the dilute limit cases, we find that a sizable phonon dispersion has a substantial effect on the system’s ground and excited state properties. Specifically, we observe that a dispersion that softens modes at q ≈ 2kF enhances electron binding. Notably, our calculated binding energies are larger than those obtained in the extended Hubbard model with an attractive nearest-neighbor interaction, which was recently proposed as a low-energy effective model for 1D cuprate chains [31] and ladders [32, 33]. The enhanced binding occurs in the singlet channel and produces a spin gap in the system; however, upon further analysis, we find that the binding does not lead to enhanced superconducting correlations.

Summary & Discussion — We have studied the 1D doped HSSH model with dispersive optical phonons with DMRG. Particularly, we have presented results for the GS and dynamical properties of the system with varying e-ph coupling and finite phonon dispersion in the strongly correlated limit ( U = 8t). We have shown that a positive (negative) phonon dispersion ( Ω′/Ω) results in a soft (hard) phonon mode near the BOW ordering vector q = 2kF, which has a direct effect on the system’s lattice displacements and enhancement of the bond correlations in the system. Previous studies involving the SSH interaction with Einstein phonons in the dilute limit reveal that SSH phonons can mediate attractive interaction between carriers, leading to small and lightweight bipolarons [7, 8, 41]. Here, we find that even in the light to moderate hole-doped limit, the system’s BE decreases with increasing Ω′/Ω, which indicates that strong phonon-mediated attractive interaction persists up to carrier concentrations relevant to synthesized doped cuprate chain compounds [31]. The increased carrier binding occurs in the singlet channel and results 5in robust BOW correlations and a spin gap in the magnetic excitation spectrum, which can be measured experimentally with inelastic neutron scattering (INS), resonant inelastic x-ray scattering (RIXS) [49, 50], or other techniques. However, for our parameter regime, the charge sector remains gapless [40] and the SC cor-relations remain weak. Overall, our results reveal that dispersive optical phonons provide an additional degree of control over binding and spin gap formation for 1D correlated systems with SSH interaction. These results also indicate that it may be necessary to go beyond the Einstein phonon approximation when modeling correlated materials.

Supplementary Note 1. Critical electron-phonon coupling for the doped HSSH model with dispersive optical phonons 

The modulation of the hopping integral in the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger (SSH) model is derived from a linear ap-proximation for the distance dependence of the overlap integral. We can, therefore, define an effective hopping as 

teff ≈ −t + g⟨Xi − Xi+1⟩ [1]. For a strong electron-phonon ( e-ph) coupling, the average lattice displacements tend to increase and can become large enough to change the sign of teff . When this occurs, the system is unstable toward dimerization and the linear SSH model becomes unphysical [1, 2]. The critical coupling gc for such sign inversions can be determined by monitoring the average value of the single-particle hopping at the chain’s center [1].
