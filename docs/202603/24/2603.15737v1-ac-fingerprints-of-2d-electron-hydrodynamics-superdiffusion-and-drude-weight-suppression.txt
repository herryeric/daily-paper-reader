## AC Fingerprints of 2D Electron Hydrodynamics: Superdiffusion and Drude Weight Suppression 

Davis Thuillier 1 and Thomas Scaffidi 1, вҲ—

> 1

Department of Physics, University of California, Irvine, Irvine, CA 92697, USA 

(Dated: 16 March 2026) Clean two-dimensional Fermi liquids are now known to exhibit an intermediate tomographic 

regime, between ballistic and NavierвҖ“Stokes transport, caused by the anomalously slow relaxation of parity-odd multipolar deformations of the Fermi surface. Here we show that this anomaly ex-tends to the dynamical realm. Starting from a microscopic numerical evaluation of the linearized electronвҖ“electron collision operator, we find that the finite-frequency nonlocal conductivity is con-trolled at low frequency by a single hydrodynamic pole, Пғ(q, Пү ) = D(q)/(iПү + О·вӢҶqz ), with dynamical exponent z = 4 /3 and superdiffusive viscosity О·вӢҶ. Remarkably, the pole residue itself is scale de-pendent and obeys D(q) вҲј qвҲ’Оұ with Оұ = 1 /3, so the dynamical properties are described by two separate exponents rather than one. We interpret the residue suppression using a Krylov-chain description of current relaxation: as q increases, the longest-lived quasinormal mode ceases to be a nearly pure current excitation and spreads over higher odd angular harmonics. Finally, we show that AC transport in narrow channels provides a direct route to measuring the exponents z and Оұ

separately. 

Hydrodynamics is a powerful framework for describing interacting many-body systems, and recent progress in ultra-clean metals has shown its relevance to electronic transport. In this regime, momentum-conserving elec-tron collisions dominate over momentum-relaxing ones, allowing viscosity to control transport. This possibility has driven substantial theoretical [1вҖ“30] and experimen-tal [31вҖ“43] activity. It is by now well understood that viscous effects can be observed in certain metals through size-dependent transport, as captured by the non-local conductivity Пғ(q). A fundamental question raised by these developments is whether microscopic quantum kinetics can produce emergent hydrodynamic theories that differ qualitatively from the NavierвҖ“Stokes paradigm. Low-dimensional sys-tems provide natural settings for such behavior. In quan-tum spin chains, for example, spin and energy transport can become superdiffusive [44вҖ“49], with a dynamical ex-ponent z lying between the ballistic value z = 1 and the diffusive value z = 2. Two-dimensional Fermi liquids provide another strik-ing example. Because of the special form of Pauli blocking in two dimensions, parity-odd angular harmon-ics of the quasiparticle distribution near the Fermi sur-face relax parametrically more slowly than parity-even ones [17, 18, 21вҖ“23, 25вҖ“29]. As a result, clean 2D Fermi liquids exhibit two distinct regimes of hydrodynamic be-havior: at large length scales, 2D Fermi liquids behave like a conventional viscous fluid described by the Navier-Stokes equation with non-local transverse conductivity 

Пғ(q) вҲј qвҲ’2; at intermediate scales, however, they enter a tomographic regime with fractional scaling Пғ(q) вҲј qвҲ’5/3

due to the long-lived odd modes [50]. Here we are interested in how these hydrodynamic ef-fects extend to the dynamical realm, which is captured by the finite-frequency non-local conductivity Пғ(q, Пү ). In the Navier-Stokes regime, going to finite frequency is fairly trivial: one finds a diffusive pole Пғ(q, Пү ) =1/(iПү + О·q 2), and the dynamical exponent z = 2 could thus have been inferred directly from the static conduc-tivity decaying as Пғ(q, 0) вҲј qвҲ’2. By contrast, we will show that the dynamical conductivity in the tomographic regime is considerably richer than its static limit sug-gests. We find that the low-frequency transverse conduc-tivity is governed by a pole of the form, 

Пғ(q, Пү ) = D(q)

iПү + О·вӢҶqz , (1) with dynamical exponent z = 4 /3 and superdiffusive vis-cosity О·вӢҶ. Crucially, the residue D(q), which can be in-terpreted as a finite-q Drude weight, is itself scale depen-dent: 

D(q) вҲј qвҲ’Оұ, Оұ = 13 . (2) The static scaling Пғ(q, 0) вҲј qвҲ’5/3 therefore arises from the combination of a superdiffusive decay rate and an anomalously suppressed residue. The tomographic regime is thus characterized by two exponents, z and Оұ,rather than one. We first compute the spectrum of the linearized electronвҖ“electron collision operator and verify the strong evenвҖ“odd hierarchy of relaxation rates characteristic of 2D Fermi liquids. We then reformulate the finite-q

current dynamics as a diffusion-dissipation model on a Krylov chain, which yields the prediction of Eq. (1). Next, we confirm these predictions with numerical cal-culations and we show how logarithmic corrections to the even decay rates govern the slow approach of the exponents z and Оұ to their asymptotic values. Finally, we show how to use AC transport in narrow channels to probe these effects experimentally.

> Appendix A: Computing the spectrum of the collision operator L

Applying the method we introduced in [30], we performed a direct calculation of the linearized electron-electron collision operator for a single parabolic band with short-range repulsion. The collision integral kernel L(k1, k2) can be expressed in terms of energy-angle coordinates. Due to rotational symmetry and mirror symmetry about ( k + kвҖІ)/2, the kernel is an even function of the angular difference, Оё = Оё1 вҲ’ Оё2 between the momenta. For each harmonic, we can then integrate out this angular dependence to define Lm(Оө, Оө вҖІ) = R dОё cos( mОё )L(Оө, Оө вҖІ, Оё ). In practice, momenta are sampled on an energy-angle grid with nОө points sampled along the energy direction and nОё points sampled in angle. 

Lm is thus represented by an nОө Г— nОө matrix. We define the rate Оіm to be the minimal magnitude eigenvalue of the Lm matrix. It is possible to replace the scalar rates in the continued fraction method of Nazaryan and Levitov [50] with the Lm matrices and evaluate the nonlocal conductivity by taking the inner product вҹЁv(Оө)|ЛҶО“q |v(Оө)вҹ© where ЛҶО“q denotes the corresponding operator continued fraction .
