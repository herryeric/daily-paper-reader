(Dated: March 25, 2026) Emergent fluxes play a central role in the low-energy properties of quantum spin liquids (QSLs), where they encode the underlying gauge structure and fractionalization of spins. Here, we show that the quadrupole moment of magnetic impurities provides a direct probe of these flux configurations in QSLs. Employing the SO(6) Majorana representation for spin-3/2 impurity operators in the isotropic Kitaev spin liquid together with a self-consistent mean-field approximation for impurity-related terms, we show that the ground-state flux sector can be identified by discontinuous jumps of the impurity quadrupole moment at the flux sector transition points. We also demonstrate that the quadrupole correlations between impurities under a magnetic field exhibit exponential decay, with decay rates that depend sensitively on the flux sector. Furthermore, we discuss the stability of π-fluxes bound to impurities with respect to model parameters and internal flux configurations, and relate our findings to Lieb’s conjecture on flux configurations. These results establish the quadrupole moments of magnetic impurities as a sensitive tool to study fractionalized excitations and flux physics in Kitaev magnets. 

INTRODUCTION 

Gauge fluxes play a central role in many quantum sys-tems and can often be observed through their coupling to external fields, with their effects appearing as geometric phases in quantum mechanics [1]. For example, magnetic fluxes can be detected through electromagnetic responses such as the Aharonov-Bohm effect or quantized vortices in superconductors. In quantum spin liquids (QSLs), however, emergent gauge fluxes do not directly couple to either electric or magnetic fields, making their detection more challenging. QSLs provide a natural setting in which emergent gauge fields arise from the fractionalization of spin de-grees of freedom [2–4]. In these systems, conventional magnetic order is absent, and the spin degrees of free-dom fractionalize into emergent quasiparticles coupled to gauge fields. The corresponding gauge fluxes distin-guish different sectors of the spin liquid and determine its low-energy properties. A paradigmatic example is the Kitaev model on the honeycomb lattice [5], which provides an exactly solv-able realization of a QSL without relying on mean-field approximations [6]. The emergent gauge field is Z2 val-ued, and each plaquette hosts a discrete flux wp = ±1, corresponding to 0 or π-flux through a plaquette of the lattice. In the pristine model without any defects, the ground state is in the zero-flux sector where all plaque-ttes exhibit wp = +1. Furthermore, the system becomes a non-Abelian spin liquid once the time-reversal sym-metry (TRS) is broken, and the excited π-fluxes in this phase can bind localized Majorana zero modes (MZMs), reflecting the nontrivial topology of the fractionalized ex-citations. The Kitaev model provides a particularly transpar-ent framework for studying the interplay between defects and gauge fluxes in the ground state. This has moti-vated a wide range of studies of lattice defects, includ-ing exactly solvable cases such as vacancies, bond disor-der, and amorphous models [7–23], as well as situations involving higher-spin impurities [24–28], mixed-spin lat-tices [29, 30], and crystallographic defects [31, 32]. Even when exact solvability is lost, these systems retain the es-sential ingredients of the Kitaev framework, namely the presence of local conserved quantities that protect the defining features of the spin-liquid state [33]. Despite these advances, directly accessing the local flux configuration remains challenging. Flux degrees of free-dom in QSLs couple only indirectly to conventional ob-servables, making their experimental detection difficult. In principle, information about the flux sector can be inferred from the dynamical spin response by resolving spectral features associated with different flux configu-rations [34–44]. However, such approaches rely on the coupling between itinerant quasiparticles and the back-ground gauge field, and therefore provide only indirect signatures rather than direct probes of the gauge flux itself. This situation motivates the search for alterna-tive observables that are more directly sensitive to the local flux environment, while still remaining experimen-tally accessible. Insights into how fluxes influence static observables have been obtained in studies of lattice defects and im-purities in QSLs [7, 8, 10, 13–15, 18, 25, 26, 31, 45– 48]. Defects locally modify the gauge sector and may bind flux excitations, allowing their local response to re-flect the surrounding flux configuration. For example, in the Kitaev model a vacancy can bind a π-flux in the ground-state sector and induce low-energy bound states [7]. Such defect-bound modes influence the local mag-

There-fore, static observables associated with defects and impu-rities, such as their field and temperature dependence or the presence of localized states, can provide signatures that distinguish different flux sectors. In this work, we investigate how larger-S magnetic im-purities can serve as local probes of the emergent flux de-grees of freedom in the Kitaev spin liquid. Building on re-cent results showing that magnetic impurities, in particu-lar spin-3 /2 moments, can bind Z2 fluxes and host MZMs in a magnetic field [28], we analyze their static multipo-lar response in the spin-liquid environment. Focusing on spin-3 /2 impurities, we find that their quadrupole mo-ments exhibit sharp discontinuities at flux-sector transi-tions. In addition, we demonstrate that quadrupole cor-relations between impurities under a magnetic field ex-hibit exponential decay with decay rates that depend on the flux sector. We also discuss the stability of impurity-bound π-fluxes as a function of the model parameters, as well as their relation to Lieb’s conjecture on flux config-urations [49–51]. To analyze these effects, we proceed as follows. We first introduce the model, then define the associated flux oper-ators, reformulate the system using Majorana fermions, and finally treat impurity-induced interactions within a mean-field approximation.

Mean-field decomposition 

We employ a mean-field decomposition technique to analyze the interacting Majorana terms originating from terms ˆHΛ and ˆHκ. Since both contributions contain terms localized at the impurity sites, we introduce a set of real-space mean-field parameters defined separately for each impurity site. Our mean-field decomposition involves only operators composed of matter fermions, with the static gauge fields around each impurity site treated exactly and left un-renormalized. For the mean-field parameters defined on the impurity site j ∈ Λ, we have: 

τ xj ≡ ⟨ iγ yj γzj ⟩, τ yj ≡ ⟨ iγ zj γxj ⟩, τ zj ≡ ⟨ iγ xj γyj ⟩, (22) which describes pair-condensations between three flavors of matter Majoranas within the same impurity site. Ad-ditionally, we have other mean-fields that represent inter-site couplings: 

T μj,k ≡ ⟨ iγ μj ck⟩ (j ∈ Λ, k ∈ ∂I j ), (23) 

tk,l ≡ ⟨ ic kcl⟩ (k, l ∈ ∂I j ). (24) It is important to note that we do not include any mean-field coupling between matter and gauge Majo-ranas. Such terms would mix the itinerant and gauge sectors and destroy the presence of exact static Z2 gauge fields in the vicinity of impurities. Details are presented in the Methods section. 

Quadrupole moments 

Having established the mean-field framework, we now analyze local observables associated with the impurity sites. The pseudo-orbital operators ˆT μ introduced in Eq. (10) represent the quadrupolar degrees of freedom of the spin-3 /2 impurity. Their expectation values are evalu-ated in the self-consistent mean-field ground state. In particular, we focus on the symmetric rank-2 quadrupole moments, which are the lowest-order time-reversal-even observables capable of distinguishing different flux sec-tors and revealing the local symmetry of the impurity state. The corresponding quadrupole components are de-fined as 

Qx ≡ ⟨ ˆT x⟩ = −τ x, Qz ≡ ⟨ ˆT z ⟩ = −τ z . (25) These symmetric quadrupole components Qx and Qz can acquire finite expectation values, regardless of the flux sector. In addition to Qx and Qz , one may also define the off-diagonal quadrupole components Qxy , Qyz , and Qzx ,corresponding to the expectation values of the operators

The resulting Majorana hopping lattice thus forms a planar and bipartite graph, to which Lieb’s conjecture is applicable, and exhibits an emergent square loop in the vicinity of the impurity site. In the bound-flux sector, no additional fluxes appear in the surrounding hexagonal plaquettes, fully consistent with Lieb’s conjecture. In contrast, in the zero-flux sec-tor the deformed hexagonal plaquette also hosts a flux, indicating a deviation from the conjecture. Finally, we comment on the situation in clusters that do not preserve reflection symmetry. As briefly discussed in the previous section, the quadrupole moment Qx ∼

iγ y γz can become finite, though still very small, in the bound-flux sector of a cluster that lacks global reflection symmetry. In this situation, the emergent square loop is further split into two square subcircuits. We confirm that one of these sub-plaquettes binds the flux, while the other does not. 

> DISCUSSION

In this work, we have shown that spin-3/2 magnetic impurities provide an effective local probe of emergent flux degrees of freedom in the Kitaev spin liquid. We demonstrated that impurity quadrupole moments encode detailed information about the surrounding flux config-uration and flux sector transitions. Using the SO(6) Majorana representation combined with a self-consistent mean-field treatment, we identified flux sector transi-tions through discontinuous changes in the impurity quadrupole moments. We further analyzed the effects of SIA and external magnetic fields, revealing reentrant and field-induced flux sector transitions, as well as the emergence of localized MZMs in the bound-flux sector. In addition, quadrupole correlations between impurities in a finite field exhibit exponential decay with flux-sector-dependent decay rates, reflecting the presence or absence of MZMs. Our analysis also highlights the important role of sym-metry. In clusters that preserve reflection symmetry, the optimized Majorana hopping lattice becomes planar and bipartite, allowing a direct connection to Lieb’s con-jecture and stabilizing impurity-bound π-fluxes. More generally, our results establish impurity quadrupole mo-ments as sensitive probes of emergent fluxes and fraction-alized excitations in Kitaev spin liquids. From an experimental perspective, detecting magnetic quadrupole moments remains challenging, because they do not couple linearly to either electric or magnetic fields. Nevertheless, several theoretical studies have discussed possible probes of quadrupolar order in spin systems, in-cluding neutron [68], Raman [69], and resonant inelastic x-ray scattering [70], as well as nuclear magnetic reso-nance (NMR) experiments [71–74]. While these tech-niques have primarily been discussed in the context of bulk quadrupolar order, it is an interesting open question whether similar approaches could detect quadrupolar re-sponses associated with dilute magnetic impurities. 12
