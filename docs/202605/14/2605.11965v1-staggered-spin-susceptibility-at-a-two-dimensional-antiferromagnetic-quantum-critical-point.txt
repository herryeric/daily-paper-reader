## Staggered spin susceptibility at a two-dimensional antiferromagnetic quantum critical point 

Yutaka Itoh 

Department of Physics, Graduate School of Science, Kyoto Sangyo University, Kamigamo-Motoyama, Kita-ku, Kyoto 603-8555, Japan 

(Dated: May 13, 2026) We report on the finite temperature staggered spin susceptibility χ(Q) as a function of the mode-mode coupling constant y1 in the self-consistent renormalization theory of two-dimensional anti-ferromagnetic spin fluctuations with zero-point quantum fluctuations just at the quantum critical point ( y0 = 0). We find that the value y1 = 0.1 is a criterion to classify the effect of the zero-point spin fluctuations on the temperature dependence of χ(Q) into a Curie law for weak y1 < 0.1 and a Curie-Weiss type or a power law type for strong y1 > 0.1. 

I. INTRODUCTION 

The zero-point spin fluctuations play a significant role in the quantum critical phenomena of itinerant electron magnetism at a two-dimensional antiferromagnetic quan-tum critical point (QCP) [1–4]. The two-dimensional quantum critical behavior of the staggered spin suscepti-bility χ(Q) ( Q is an antiferromagnetic wave vector) is a logarithmic temperature dependence near absolute zero temperature [1–5]. The critical region is too narrow. The logarithmic divergence of χ(Q) holds only at extremely low temperatures [1–5]. The finite temperature χ(Q) at the QCP is of a Curie-Weiss type in the self-consistent renormalization (SCR) theory with zero-point quantum fluctuations [1, 2, 4]. The logarithmic divergence near absolute zero temperature and the finite temperature Curie-Weiss law are characteristic of χ(Q) at the two-dimensional antiferromagnetic QCP in the SCR theory with the zero-point fluctuations. These are different from a Curie law for χ(Q) at the QCP in the SCR theory with-out the zero-point fluctuations [6]. Although the numerical χ(Q) is called ”Curie-Weiss behavior” [1, 2, 4], it has been unclear how the Curie-Weiss law is parameterized in the SCR theory with the zero-point fluctuations. The Curie-Weiss law of χ(Q) in nearly antiferromagnetic systems without the zero-point fluctuations [6] was expressed in terms of the SCR pa-rameters [7]. Phenomenological simple expressions of 

χ(Q) are convenient for experimental estimations of the spin fluctuation parameters. It is crucial for experimen-tal studies whether χ(Q) is a Curie law or a Curie-Weiss law, because the absence of the finite Curie-Weiss tem-perature has served as the identification of the QCP [7–9]. In this paper, according to the Watanabe-Miyake SCR theory with the zero-point spin fluctuations [1], we study the SCR equation of χ(Q) as a function of the mode-mode coupling constant y1 at the two-dimensional anti-ferromagnetic QCP. We find that the effect of the zero-point spin fluctuations decreases with decreasing the value of y1. The temperature dependence of χ(Q) at relevant temperature region depend on the value of y1.We show that χ(Q) changes from a Curie law for weak 

y1 < 0.1 to a Curie-Weiss type or a power law type for strong y1 > 0.1.

V. CONCLUSION 

In conclusion, the effect of the zero-point quantum fluc-tuations on the temperature dependence of the staggered spin susceptibility χ(Q) depends on the magnitude of the mode-mode coupling constant y1 at the two-dimensional antiferromagnetic QCP. In the weakly coupling regime (y1 < 0.1), the predominant thermal spin fluctuations lead to a Curie law of χ(Q). In the strongly coupling regime ( y1 > 0.1), the zero-point spin fluctuations have an influence comparable to that of the thermal fluctua-tions in determining χ(Q). As to experimental studies, the absence of the Curie-Weiss temperature ( tθ = 0) can 4serve as the identification of the QCP ( y0 = 0) for the weak mode-mode coupling systems ( y1 < 0.1).
